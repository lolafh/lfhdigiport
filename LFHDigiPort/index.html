Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

From the design beginnings portion of this course, I have learned that the words you pick and how you display them have a large impact on accessibility. In design, you have to balance being clear and concise but also creative and unique. I don't know if you can call my work so far that, but I did try very hard to make it at the very least accessible!

2. Leverage Design Principles.

What I have learned about design principles so far is from what has happened when I don't follow them. When I just go with whatever looks good to me without thinking about the technical aspect of anything, it is not consumable for the public. For example, when I designed Logo A, I thought it was my best one because I liked it, but I did not think about principles as much as I should. It made the work inaccessible and honestly? Slightly boring. According to my UX participants, anyway.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I engaged with others in a workshop type environment in class whne out pieces weren't perfect yet. Not that any piece is perfect, but you understand what I mean. Specfically, my peers were helpful when I didn't understand what questions to ask my participants for my UX study or how to ask them, and how they may impact the outcome of my logo.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Practicing revision has been the hardest part for me, as I do not like anyone but myself critcizing me or my work. But, it has made me a stronger creator. In the Good Design project, I used too many prepositional phrases, and had to go back and redo all the text in order to make the writing more professional. That project has been the most meticilous of the short design section, as it took lots of revising to make sure all the small things looked nice so the piece overall looked nice.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presenting has never been a hard task for me, but presenting unfinshed work is new. There is something very vulnerable about it that you can't rehearse or prepare yourself for. But, it makes the process more rewarding.
